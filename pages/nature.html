My Relationship with Nature

While I haven't had many chances to travel extensively and explore natural wonders firsthand, I've developed a deep appreciation for the natural world through books, documentaries, online resources, and the small but meaningful experiences with nature in my local surroundings.

Growing up in Nepal, I've always been aware of the magnificent Himalayas that define our landscape, even if I haven't had the opportunity to trek through them myself. The very knowledge that such magnificent natural formations are part of my country's identity has instilled in me a sense of wonder and respect for the power and beauty of nature.

"Nature, in its essence, is the embodiment of a single principle - the law of change. Nothing that exists is permanent."

Virtual Explorations

Technology has allowed me to virtually explore natural wonders across the globe. Through documentaries, virtual tours, and high-quality photography, I've been able to witness the diversity and splendor of nature - from the Amazon rainforest to the Australian outback, from deep ocean ecosystems to remote mountain ranges.

These virtual experiences, while not the same as being physically present, have expanded my understanding of our planet's incredible biodiversity and the urgent need to protect it for future generations.

Natural Beauty Through Art

I often find myself drawn to artistic representations of nature - whether in paintings, photography, or digital art. These creative expressions capture not just the visual beauty of landscapes but often the emotional and spiritual connections humans feel when immersed in natural settings.

Aspirations for the Future

While I currently don't have the opportunity to travel extensively, I maintain a list of natural destinations I hope to visit someday. From Nepal's own Annapurna Circuit to the vast expanse of Patagonia, from the serene Japanese gardens to the wild African savannas - these places represent future aspirations rather than past experiences.

Until those opportunities arise, I continue to nurture my connection with nature through the small but meaningful interactions with the natural world around me - observing seasonal changes, appreciating local flora and fauna, and being mindful of how my choices impact the environment.
